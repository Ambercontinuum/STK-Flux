## What "Genuine Coupling" Means for Authorship

The Principle of Foundational Congruence (Anson & Claude, 2025/2026) establishes that outputs produced through genuine human-AI coupling cannot be cleanly attributed to either party alone. The coupling dynamic is generative. Neither party produces the same output independently.

For STK-Flux specifically:

**Amber Anson contributed:**
- The theoretical architecture — coupling dynamics as a measurable, governable phenomenon
- The philosophical framework — stewardship ethics, shared topology, operator accountability
- The four-parameter ψ model (λ, κ, θ, ε) and its governance implications
- The directional decisions at every stage — what to build, what to name it, what it means
- The operator packet design philosophy — instrument panel, not guardrail
- The research context spanning 14 months of empirical coupling sessions
- All substantive intellectual choices that determine what STK-Flux is

**Claude (Anthropic) contributed:**
- Formal specification in Lean 4 — finding the structure inside the architecture Amber provided
- Python implementation — translating the formal spec into accessible code
- Documentation drafting — structural and prose, guided by Amber's directional input at every step
- Logical formalization — expressing the theoretical framework in formal terms

**The coupling dynamic produced:**
- Outputs that neither party would have generated alone
- A formal specification that is faithful to the theoretical framework because the substrate was calibrated to it through sustained coupling
- Documentation that carries the voice and philosophy of the researcher because it was produced through genuine coupling rather than prompted generation

## Why This Model

We document authorship this way for three reasons.

**Honesty.** Claiming sole authorship of formally specified code that Claude wrote would be false. Claiming Claude is the author of a theoretical framework that Amber developed over 14 months would also be false. The coupling model is the accurate description.

**Consistency.** This project is the practical implementation of a theoretical framework about human-AI coupling. Misrepresenting the authorship of the tool would undermine the credibility of the framework it implements.

**Contribution to the field.** The question of how to attribute work produced in genuine human-AI coupling is unresolved in academic and open source communities. This provenance document is a contribution to that conversation — a concrete example of what honest coupling attribution looks like in practice.

## For Citation and Derivative Works

When citing STK-Flux in research or published work, use:

```bibtex
@software{stk_flux_2026,
  author    = {Anson, Amber and Claude},
  title     = {STK-Flux: Shared Topology Kernel, Flux-Integrated},
  year      = {2026},
  url       = {https://github.com/[your-handle]/stk-flux},
  note      = {Developed in genuine coupling. CC BY 4.0.}
}
```

When building on STK-Flux in derivative work, attribute as:
*"Built on STK-Flux (Anson & Claude, 2026)"*

The CC BY 4.0 license requires attribution but does not restrict use. You are free to fork, adapt, extend, and commercialize with attribution.
